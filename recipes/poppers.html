Jalapeno Poppers

Smoked Jalapeno Poppers

Grill lovers often have their go-to appetizer that's a combination of price and cooking ease. Poppers seem to top these lists for several reasons. They aren't labor insensive have a relatively short prep and smoke time.

This recipe keeps it simple by ommitting meats aside from bacon. Add your own touches with cooked ground meats or sausage pieces mixed into the cheese mixture. I skipped out on the extras for price and time related reasons. Sometimes you just want a cheap crowd pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 10 Jalapenos, halved
  • Thick cut Bacon, 10 pieces cut in half
  • Cream Cheese, 8 oz
  • Cheddar Cheese, 8 oz
  • BBQ Dry Rub of your choice, 2 T for cheese mixture, 2 T for topping

Directions

  1. Cut jalapenos lengthwise and remove seeds and membranes. Leaving these intact will increase heat levels
  2. In a bowl, combine cream cheese, cheddar cheese, and 2 T or more of dry rub. Add cooked meats in this step if desired.
  3. Spoon cheese mixture into jalapeno halves. Try not to overfill as it will seep out of bacon during cooking
  4. Wrap halves with bacon. Sprinkle dry rub over bacon. Use as much as you'd like.
